17. SPECULATIVE INTERPRETATION

This document fixes intuitive estimates and draft versions of the interpretation of the Theta–Psi model. These are sketches requiring consistency checks. Many, if not all, of these ideas may be erroneous.

ON THE POSSIBILITY OF A SIMULTANEOUS BEGINNING WITHOUT SINGULARITY
A scenario in which the Theta and Psi fields existed independently until a certain moment. The moment of their "intersection" could lead to the start of dynamics in the entire volume at once. To explain the isotropy of the cosmic microwave background, it is necessary to assume a period of extremely tight coupling of the fields, during which, due to intensive energy exchange, the system managed to reach local thermal equilibrium (thermalize).

REFRACTIVE INTERPRETATION OF REDSHIFT
The evolution of the global index n(t) can be an alternative to the metric expansion of space. In such a formulation, the observed redshift z is a consequence of the change in time scale in the medium during the passage of a photon. This requires meeting the conditions of the Tolman test: n(t) must universally affect the rate of all physical processes, mimicking the expansion effect without changing coordinate distances. The problem of sky brightness in a static space (Olbers' paradox) may be linked to dissipative energy losses in the medium at large distances.

ENERGY EXCHANGE WITH THE PSI SUBSTRATE
Matter can be viewed as an open system interacting with Psi. In this case, vacuum effects (Casimir, Lamb shift) are manifestations of this exchange. A mandatory condition remains the covariant closure of the full Theta, Psi, Matter system to satisfy the Bianchi identities.

CHIRALITY AND TOPOLOGICAL CONNECTIVITY OF THE SUBSTRATE
The possibility that elementary particles are not just localizations of energy but topological defects (solitons) in the complex Psi field is considered. In such an interpretation, their fundamental characteristics, such as spin and charge, are determined by the topological invariants of the field. Spontaneous symmetry breaking at the moment of field coupling could fix a non-zero mean helicity of the vacuum (topological twist). This would allow explaining the fundamental asymmetry of the world (e.g., neutrino chirality) as a consequence of the internal connectivity of the substrate.

The main technical limitation remains the absence of observed vacuum birefringence. For the hypothesis to be viable, the chiral contribution to the Lagrangian must be dynamically screened in the low-energy limit, appearing only in anomalous effects at extreme Theta gradients or in the early Universe.

MASS DRIFT
If mass is a projection of Psi, its slow drift over time is possible. The upper limit of the rate of such a change (10^-10 per year) is dictated by the precision of modern measurements of atomic transition frequencies.
