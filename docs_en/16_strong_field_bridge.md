16. STRONG FIELD BRIDGE

This document fixes the step-by-step derivation of the link between background parameters and the characteristics of compact objects in the Theta-Psi hypothesis.

1. FORMULATION OF THE STATIONARY PROBLEM
A spherically symmetric region around a compact source is considered. The Theta (time) and Psi (energy) fields depend only on the radius r.
Stationary radial functional of the system:
L_stat = 1/2 I_grad - U(theta, Psi) + alpha F(J_refr)
where:
I_grad = (theta')^2 + w_Psi (Psi')^2 - inhomogeneity invariant.
J_refr = Phi_eff(theta) I_grad - argument of the refractive response.
Phi_eff(theta) = ln(theta/theta_0) - logarithmic potential of deviation from the background.

2. EULER-LAGRANGE EQUATIONS
Variation with respect to the fields gives a system of quasi-linear equations:
1/r^2 d/dr [ r^2 A(r) theta' ] = - dU/d_theta + alpha F'(J) (1/theta) I_grad
1/r^2 d/dr [ r^2 A(r) w_Psi Psi' ] = dU/d_Psi
The function A(r) = 1 + 2 alpha Phi_eff F'(J) defines the effective conductivity of the vacuum.

3. WEAK-FIELD ASYMPTOTICS (MATCHING)
At large distances (r -> infinity):
I_grad -> 0, Phi_eff -> 0, consequently A(r) -> 1.
The equations transition into the standard Poisson form:
Delta theta = Source.
Solution: theta(r) = theta_0 (1 - r_g/r).
This links the Theta gradient at infinity with the Newtonian mass M (via the radius r_g).
The refractive index in this limit: n = 1 + 2 Phi_eff = 1 + 2 ln(1 - r_g/r) = 1 + 2 Phi_N.

4. EMERGENCE OF THE BRIDGE SCALE (BRIDGE SCALE)
When approaching the source, the gradient (theta')^2 grows. The influence of the refractive sector (alpha) becomes significant when the term 2 alpha Phi_eff F' becomes comparable to unity.
From the structure of J_refr, the emergence of a natural length scale r_c follows:
r_c^2 ≈ alpha / V0
This scale defines the region in which non-linear Theta-Psi effects begin to dominate over classical gravity, preventing collapse into a singularity.

In the current version, the stationary strong-field regime is considered closed as an effective problem.

Within this formulation, the following have been obtained:
- finite refractive index;
- absence of infinite redshift;
- existence of an outgoing light characteristic;
- matching with the weak field at large distances;
- regular behavior of solutions.

The following are not yet closed:
- dynamical collapse;
- formation of a compact object from arbitrary initial conditions;
- full non-stationary regime.

5. SATURATION AND ABSENCE OF HORIZON
Due to the exponential saturation F(J) = 1 - exp(-J), the derivative F'(J) tends to zero as gradients grow.
This leads to the fact that the vacuum conductivity A(r) stops growing, and the value of the Theta field in the center remains finite and positive:
lim(r->0) theta(r) = theta_c > 0.
Consequence: the refractive index n remains finite, which excludes the formation of a mathematical event horizon.

RESULT
The strong field bridge is a direct consequence of the dynamics of the effective vacuum conductivity A(r). It links mass (far field), coupling parameter alpha (response intensity), and potential V0 (substrate elasticity), completely determining the structure of compact objects without involving external assumptions.
